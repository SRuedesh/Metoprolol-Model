### 2.2.1 In vitro and physicochemical data

Drug-dependent input parameters for the metoprolol enantiomers were taken from the published metoprolol PBPK model and the cited primary sources therein [[1](References.md)]. The key physicochemical properties include molecular weight, pK<sub>a</sub>, lipophilicity, solubility of metoprolol tartrate and succinate, fraction unbound in plasma and intestinal permeability. The model uses the same physicochemical properties for the two enantiomers where no enantiomer-specific difference is supported by the source data.

CYP2D6 K<sub>m</sub> values for α-hydroxylation and O-demethylation are based on in vitro data by Mautz et al. [[15](References.md)] as summarized in the metoprolol model publication [[1](References.md)]. activity-score-dependent k<sub>cat</sub> values were optimized in fixed pathway ratios and are documented in the model publication [[1](References.md), Table 3].

| Parameter group | Model use | Main source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Molecular weight, pK<sub>a</sub>, lipophilicity, solubility and f<sub>u</sub> | Parent-enantiomer physicochemical and distribution inputs | [Rüdesheim et al. 2020](References.md), Table 2 and cited primary sources |
| CYP2D6 K<sub>m</sub> values | Enantiomer-specific α-hydroxylation and O-demethylation | [Rüdesheim et al. 2020](References.md), Table 2 |
| CYP2D6 activity-score-dependent k<sub>cat</sub> values | DGI model scaling across CYP2D6 activity scores | [Rüdesheim et al. 2020](References.md), Table 3 |
| Formulation parameters | Normal-release tartrate and controlled-release succinate absorption | [Rüdesheim et al. 2020](References.md), Table 2 |

### 2.2.2 Clinical data

Clinical plasma concentration-time data were used for model building and verification after intravenous infusion and oral metoprolol administration. The clinical dataset includes racemic metoprolol, enantiomer-specific metoprolol and α-hydroxymetoprolol observations, with CYP2D6 activity-score information used where available.

| Publication | Clinical data used |
| --- | --- |
| [Kirchheiner et al. 2004 / Seeringer et al. 2008](References.md) | Oral normal-release metoprolol tartrate data in adult CYP2D6 poor, normal and ultrarapid metabolizer groups were used to inform activity-score-dependent enantiomer metabolism. |
| [Sharma et al. 2005](References.md) | Oral metoprolol tartrate data in adult CYP2D6 poor and normal metabolizer groups supported racemic and enantiomer-specific DGI evaluation. |
| [Regårdh et al. 1974](References.md), [Regårdh et al. 1980](References.md), [Johnsson et al. 1975](References.md), [Kelly et al. 1985](References.md) and [Godbillon et al. 1985](References.md) | Intravenous infusion and oral metoprolol studies in adults supported systemic disposition, oral absorption and metabolite formation across the model-building dataset. |
| [Werner et al. 2003](References.md), [Krösser et al. 2006](References.md) and [Jin et al. 2008](References.md) | Oral metoprolol tartrate studies with metoprolol and α-hydroxymetoprolol measurements supported parent-metabolite evaluation and activity-score-dependent predictions. |
| [Huang et al. 1999](References.md), [Hamelin et al. 2000](References.md), [Johnson et al. 1996](References.md), [Luzier et al. 1999](References.md), [Stout et al. 2011](References.md), [Parker et al. 2011](References.md), [Damy et al. 2004](References.md) and [Bae et al. 2014](References.md) | Oral metoprolol tartrate or succinate data in adults provided additional verification across CYP2D6 status groups, sex groups and formulation conditions. |
